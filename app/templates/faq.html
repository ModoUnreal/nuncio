Frequenty Asked Questions:

What is this site?

Please refer to the about page.

What do the arrows represent?

The arrows are used for voting, the up arrow being a vote for and the down arrow being against. Once voted the post's score will change according to which arrow was pressed and then either be seen and celebrated by everybody or doomed to the pits of obscurity.

What does the important button do?

Importance is an alternative to voting up. The difference here is that giving importance gives the post a much greater boost in terms of how long it stays in it's highest position and it also allows a post to move the ranks a lot more quickly. This may sound all great and all however the user that gives importance to a post does so at the expense of 5 of his own attained points. This should prevent a mass of people from giving importance to a post due to bias or political beliefs.

So when should I give importance?

I guess if you feel that a certain post or topic is not getting enough attention, that's all.

What exactly is a topic?

A topic is a like a hashtag on sites like Twitter. It's primarily used to quickly describe the general subject area a post is about. For example say a post is made about a robbery in London the topic given would be called London and maybe another one called robbery.

And this event thing?

An event is the specific event a post is about. This is used so that a number of posts about the same topic, from different sources, can be easily ordered and seen by users. For example, a post about a car crash in New York would have "Car crash in New York" as it's event name, whilst the topic tag would probably be called New York.
